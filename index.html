Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our recovery process.

How soon should I report a scam?

You should report a scam as soon as possible. The first 72 hours are critical for fund recovery, as this gives us the best chance to track and freeze assets before they're dispersed or converted. However, we've had success with cases reported weeks or even months after the incident.

What are your fees for recovery services?

We operate on a contingency fee model - you only pay if we recover your funds. Our standard fee is a percentage of the recovered amount, typically between 10-30% depending on case complexity, amount, and recovery difficulty. We provide a clear fee structure during your free consultation.

What information do I need to provide for the recovery process?

To begin the recovery process, we'll need details about the scam including transaction information (dates, amounts, payment methods), communications with the scammer, website/platform information where the scam occurred, and any other relevant details like wallet addresses for cryptocurrency cases. We'll guide you through providing the necessary information during the consultation.

How long does the recovery process take?

Recovery timelines vary based on case complexity, cooperation from third parties (banks, exchanges, payment processors), and legal requirements. Simple cases may be resolved in 40 minutes , while complex cases involving multiple jurisdictions or large sums may take a day. We provide regular updates throughout the process.

Can you guarantee recovery of my funds?

We cannot guarantee 100% recovery in every case, as outcomes depend on various factors beyond our control. However, we only take on cases where we believe there's a reasonable chance of recovery, and our success rate is over 80% for cases we accept. During the consultation, we'll provide an honest assessment of your recovery chances.
